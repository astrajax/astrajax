### What Have You Built With Agents In The Last 6 Months?

In the last six months I have built and deployed a multi-agent operating layer on top of a year-long Airtable data backbone for Butternut Box Direct Sales, then used that proof to start AstraJax.

The core pattern is bounded agents working inside real commercial workflows, with clean data, clear handoffs, human approval, and an audit trail. This is not "add a chatbot to a process". It is rebuilding the process so agents can safely do useful work inside it.

The strongest examples are:

- A Trinity-style workflow where agents link records, propose changes, route them for human approval, and then execute the approved action.
- Clive, a context and adoption layer designed to help teams use AI without losing the operating knowledge that makes the work useful.
- A feedback-to-code-fix workflow using Slack, Airtable, Hyperagent, and Cursor via MCP. Team feedback is triaged, clarified, classified, grouped with related bugs, enriched with codebase context, sent to me for approval, then passed to Cursor for implementation. Doc Albright then reports back to the team with a weekly summary.
- A broader operating layer of role-specific interfaces and agents supporting reporting, staffing, training, feedback, bug handling, and operational decision support.

The work started inside a real commercial team, not a lab. A single LinkedIn post about the agent rollout led Airtable and Hyperagent to recruit me for Airspace LA, where I was flown out as a headline speaker to show what a Hyperagent power user looks like in the wild.

### Walk Us Through What You Are Showing

The attachments show the same pattern at three levels.

Second, they show the agent layer: bounded agents with specific jobs, such as linking records, drafting proposals, triaging feedback, enriching bug reports, producing summaries, and routing decisions back to humans. ### What Does Agent-First Look Like For AstraJax In The Next 6 Months?

For AstraJax, agent-first means commercial teams stop treating AI as a side tool and start rebuilding their operating work so agents can act inside it safely.

In the next six months I want to package the Butternut pattern into a repeatable AstraJax delivery model:

- Diagnose the messy workflows and context debt blocking useful AI.
- Build the boring layer first: clean records, clear roles, human approval points, and operating memory.
- Deploy narrow agents for real jobs: feedback triage, reporting, staffing support, customer or field insight capture, training support, and improvement loops.
- Build Clive as the context layer that keeps agent work grounded: intake, review queues, source tracking, context health checks, and agent-readable operating memory.
- Keep humans in judgement roles while agents handle classification, enrichment, drafting, routing, and follow-up.

The thing competitors will miss is that the agent is not the product by itself. The system around the agent is the product. Clean context, human approval, adoption design, and audit trail are what turn clever demos into operational leverage.

### Walk Us Through What You Are Showing

The materials show a model for context-aware education and specialist support in a community setting.

The first layer is access: connectivity, power, devices, and a usable computer centre. In a commercial team, the boring layer is clean data and clear workflows. At Seeds of Promise, the boring layer is more physical and social: internet, electricity, working devices, local trust, local champions, and local ownership.

The second layer is context: local language, community goals, constraints, available resources, cultural reference points, and the practical jobs people are trying to do. This matters because generic training and generic coaching do not travel well unless they are adapted to the people using them.

The third layer is the agent toolkit: narrow helpers for learning, farming and greenhouse planning, fundraising, small-enterprise support, and community leadership. The goal is not to make local leaders technical. The goal is to bring specialist support closer to them, in a form they can actually use.

For example, greenhouse advice currently depends on word of mouth or travelling hours to speak to someone with experience. A farming support agent could coach leaders through planning, setup, maintenance, and local trade-offs using context about their resources and constraints. A fundraising agent could take a community idea written in Chichewa and help shape it into a donor-ready proposal in English. A small-enterprise agent could help a tailoring project with pricing, stock, planning, and grant applications.

The larger point is that AI can repackage outside expertise into a format that fits the local context. Even if expert support existed, it would still need translating into the community's language, constraints, and reality. That is where agents become powerful.

### What Does Agent-First Look Like For Seeds Of Promise In The Next 6 Months?

For Seeds of Promise, agent-first does not mean dropping a generic chatbot into a low-connectivity environment. It means building the foundation first, then shaping education, coaching, and specialist guidance around the community's real language, goals, constraints, and decisions.

In the next six months, the pilot should:

- Confirm the connectivity and power requirements.
- Identify the minimum equipment and local ownership model.
- Train local champions.
- Capture the community's priority needs and language context.
- Build the first agent toolkit for context-aware education, farming, fundraising, enterprise, and leadership.
- Test the agents on practical jobs: learning support, greenhouse planning, proposal writing, business planning, and meeting preparation.
- Document the playbook so the model can be tested with other Links-connected communities later.

The thing others will miss is that useful AI in this setting starts before the AI. The agent only becomes useful when the foundation, trust, and local context are in place. The real product is not a chatbot. It is contextual access to education and specialist support.
