## 1. What The Founding 500 Is Looking For

The Founding 500 is Hyperagent's $10M inference-credit programme for 500 founders and operators building agent-first companies. The offer is positioned as **$200 for $20,000 in Hyperagent inference credits**, not cash funding.

The page's core thesis:

- Most companies are still treating AI as a feature.
- The best companies are rebuilding workflows around agents.
- Hyperagent wants founders and operators with an ambitious point of view on how to agentify a business.
- It is open to both new agent-first companies and operators reimagining existing companies.

The strongest applicant profile:

- They have already put agents to work.
- They have shipped something real in the last six months.
- They can show agents in production, rebuilt workflows, and users or customers on the other end.
- They can clearly explain what agent-first looks like in their industry six months from now.
- They have a thesis, not just a pitch.

Plain read: this is unusually well matched to Matthew's story. The AstraJax application should be the main submission. Seeds of Promise should be framed as a credible second use case of the same method, with more care because it is earlier.

**Team size:** Solo founder.

**Which best describes you?** Building a new agent-first company.

### Core Answer

AstraJax helps commercial teams turn messy workflows, scattered data, and domain expertise into AI-ready operating systems. Clive is the managed context environment that keeps those systems usable by agents.

The strongest proof is the Butternut Box Direct Sales operating layer:

- Roughly a year spent building the boring layer: clean data, clear workflows, role-specific interfaces, and human approval points.
- Then a fast agent layer: 14 agents built in 11 days, used directly by about 20 office users and benefiting a wider 120-person sales operation.
- Airtable recruited Matthew after seeing this work, flew him to Airspace LA as a headline speaker, and presented him as a Hyperagent power user in the wild.
- Austin Canary described the Airspace session as one of the moments people kept referencing all day.
- The current agent pattern is not loose chatbot usage. It is bounded agents, clear handoffs, human approval, and an audit trail.

### What To Say For "Built With Agents"

Feedback is triaged, clarified, classified, grouped with related bugs, enriched with codebase context, sent to Matthew for approval, then passed to Cursor for implementation and weekly team reporting.

Keep the claim controlled:

### Six-Month Thesis

The six-month AstraJax thesis should be:

> Agent-first commercial teams will not be the ones with the most chatbots. They will be the ones whose messy operating work has been rebuilt so agents can safely act inside it.

Specific six-month build:

- Package the Butternut pattern into a repeatable AstraJax delivery model.
- Build Clive as the context layer: intake, review queue, context health checks, source tracking, and agent-readable operating memory.
- Ship narrow agent workflows for commercial teams: feedback triage, reporting, staffing support, customer or field insight capture, training support, and improvement loops.
- Keep humans in judgement roles, with agents doing the sludge: classification, enrichment, drafting, routing, and follow-up.

What competitors miss:

- The boring layer comes first.
- Personality and adoption are infrastructure, not decoration.
- Domain experts can become architects when the system gives them clean rails.

### Why Matthew

## 4. Application Two: Seeds Of Promise x AstraJax

### Strategic Reframe

Seeds is larger than a philanthropic side project. It is the clearest human version of the AstraJax thesis: fit training, coaching, and specialist support to the person and the context, then use agents to make that support available at the point of need.

The big idea is **context-aware education and specialist access**. In a commercial team, AstraJax makes AI useful by building the foundation layer first: clean workflows, trusted data, clear roles, and human approval. At Seeds of Promise, the foundation layer is more physical and social:

- Connectivity.
- Reliable electricity.
- Devices.
- Local trust.
- Local champions.
- Community language and culture.
- The economic reality people are actually operating inside.

Then the agent layer can do something much bigger than "AI training". It can repackage outside knowledge into forms the community can use: education support for young people, greenhouse and farming guidance for leaders, fundraising materials in English from ideas written in Chichewa, business planning for tailoring or local enterprise, and coaching that understands the local constraints rather than assuming a Western business context.

This should not be framed as charity theatre or a small feel-good pilot. It is a second proof of the same method:

- AstraJax for operationally heavy commercial teams.
- Seeds for communities where the missing foundation is power, connectivity, devices, and locally grounded context.

The honest caution is narrower: Seeds does not yet have production agents in the way AstraJax / Butternut does. But the opportunity is larger than "a pilot". The application should say clearly that Matthew has already proved the agent method commercially, has personally validated the Seeds need in Malawi, and is now applying the same foundation-first model to context-aware education and community support.

### Recommended Positioning

**Project name:** Seeds of Promise x AstraJax.  
**Title:** Founder, AstraJax.  
**Stage:** Pre-product, idea, or Building, not launched.

Use **Building, not launched** if we want to emphasise that Matthew has already visited, coached, identified local appetite, and started designing the programme. Use **Pre-product, idea** only if we want to be very conservative.

**Industry:** Nonprofit / government, Education, or Other.

My recommendation is **Nonprofit / government** if the form forces one category. Education can be included in the written answer.

**Team size:** Solo founder, unless including Seeds / Links delivery partners makes 2-5 more honest.

**Which best describes you?** Building a new agent-first company.

### Core Answer

Seeds of Promise x AstraJax is a model for context-aware education and specialist support in communities that are ready to move.

The goal is not simply to teach people to use AI. The goal is to build the conditions that let AI become useful: power, connectivity, devices, local champions, community trust, and a clear understanding of the language, culture, constraints, and ambitions of the people using it.

Once that foundation exists, agents can bring specialist support closer to the community:

- Young people can learn digital skills and ask questions in a way that fits their level and context.
- Village elders and community leaders can get farming and greenhouse guidance without travelling hours for word-of-mouth advice.
- Local teams can write ideas in Chichewa and turn them into donor-ready funding materials in English.
- Tailoring and local enterprise projects can get practical planning, pricing, and proposal support.
- Outside experts can have their advice repackaged into formats that actually fit the community.

The equivalent of the commercial "boring layer" is:

- Connectivity.
- Reliable power.
- Devices.
- Local champions.
- Training.
- Captured community context: language, goals, constraints, resources, and needs.

Then the agent layer can be useful:

- Learning support agent.
- Farming and greenhouse guidance agent.
- Fundraising and proposal-writing agent.
- Small-enterprise planning agent.
- Community-leadership planning agent.

The principle is: **bring the specialist closer, but fit the specialist to the local context first.**

### What To Say For "Built With Agents"

This answer should bridge Matthew's shipped agent experience with the larger Seeds vision:

- Matthew has already built bounded, approval-led agent workflows in a commercial operating system.
- Seeds is the next use case for that method, adapted to context-aware education and community support.
- He has visited Seeds of Promise in Malawi, built trust with the community and Sam at Links, delivered introductory AI coaching, and confirmed appetite for practical technology support.
- The community already has a version of a computer centre and the vision to use it. The missing layer is connectivity, electricity, devices, local champions, and context-aware tools.
- The project is now moving from training and relationship-building into a structured programme: infrastructure, context capture, local champions, first agent prototypes, and a repeatable playbook.

Be honest that the production agent proof sits in AstraJax / Butternut, while Seeds is the larger second application of the method.

### What To Show

Best evidence assets:

- Photos or evidence from the Malawi visit, if available and appropriate.
- Any screenshots or materials from the introductory AI coaching.
- The Seeds plan document.
- A simple pilot map: infrastructure -> context capture -> local champions -> narrow agents -> local use cases.
- Any early agent mockups or sample prompts for farming, fundraising, education, or leadership support.

Avoid:

- "AI in Africa" as a vague pitch.
- Charity theatre.
- Any suggestion that outside tech replaces local judgement.

### Six-Month Thesis

The six-month Seeds thesis should be:

> Agent-first community support will not mean dropping a generic chatbot into a low-connectivity environment. It will mean building the foundation first, then shaping training, coaching, and specialist guidance around the community's real language, goals, constraints, and decisions.

Specific six-month build:

- Confirm connectivity and power requirements.
- Identify minimum equipment and local ownership model.
- Train local champions.
- Capture community needs and language context.
- Build the first small agent toolkit for context-aware education and practical specialist support.
- Test the agents on practical jobs: learning support, greenhouse planning, fundraising, small enterprise, and leadership planning.
- Document the playbook for possible replication through Links-connected communities.

### Why Matthew

Use the crossover:

- Matthew has already proved the method in a complex commercial team.
- He has a direct relationship with Seeds of Promise and has been there in person.
- He understands that AI only becomes useful when fitted to context.
- He has personally felt the gap: trying to provide business coaching when the local context was too different to package the advice well.
- He can turn the agent-first thesis into a human, practical model for bringing education and specialist support closer to people who already have the ambition.
