# Seeds of Promise - AI, Tech Infrastructure, and Future Plan

**Purpose:** Capture what I have already done with Seeds of Promise, what has emerged since, and what I may do next with Sam / Links and other communities in Malawi.

---

## Executive Summary

In October 2025, while in Malawi, I was asked at short notice to run an AI coaching session with community leaders connected to Seeds of Promise. The session landed well. It was practical, contextual, and showed that AI could be made useful even for people without a technical background, especially when examples were rooted in their own setting, language, and community needs.

Since then, the idea has developed into a potential experiment: use my AI, systems, and operations skillset to help Seeds of Promise and other Links-connected communities improve access to technology, internet connectivity, and practical AI coaching.

The current hypothesis is:

> There may be a replicable model for helping underserved communities get connected, learn practical tech skills, and use AI in ways that improve education, guidance, community development, and access to support.

The immediate opportunity is not to start a business fully formed. It is to test whether there is something real here: something useful to communities, energising for me, and potentially scalable through partnerships, fundraising, and a small team of tech/AI-experienced people.

## Background

I went to Malawi in October 2025 and spent time with Seeds of Promise. While there, I was asked at short notice to run an AI coaching session with their community leaders.

The session focused on making AI understandable and useful at an entry level. The strongest parts were not abstract AI concepts, but practical examples in context:

- How AI could support education and learning.
- How prompting could be adapted to a Malawian setting.
- How local language, including Chichewa, could be part of the interaction.
- How community leaders could use AI to access guidance, structure thinking, generate materials, and support people around them.

After the session, Seeds of Promise asked about ongoing Zoom coaching. I did not follow up properly because work became extremely intense and I was still dealing with the grief of Dad's death. That is context, not an excuse, but it explains why the thread paused.

The experience stayed with me because the energy felt different. It was a direct use of skills I have built at Butternut - AI, systems, coaching, training, operational thinking - but applied to people and communities where the usefulness was immediately visible.

## What I Have Already Done

### 1. Delivered an AI coaching session at Seeds of Promise

I ran an introductory AI training session for community leaders while in Malawi.

The session was:

- Entry-level and accessible.
- Built around practical use cases rather than technical theory.
- Adapted in the moment to the local context.
- Focused on how AI could help with real community needs, not just novelty.

### 2. Created and shared the training content

After returning, I sent the training materials to Sam at Links so he could share them with Mclean and the others who attended the session.

The materials were intended to give them something concrete to revisit and build from.

### 3. Opened the door to future support

In my follow-up to Sam, I said I would be happy to support future sessions if Seeds of Promise or other Links partners wanted them.

Sam responded positively. His view was that there was real scope to use the core learning points with Links partners because many leaders do not know where to start with AI or how it can be used efficiently.

### 4. Received useful feedback from Sam

Sam's feedback was that the content was strong, pitched mostly at entry level, and relevant. His main build was that some wording and jargon would need to be adapted further for communities without an IT or computing background.

He also highlighted that success would depend on:

- Breaking the concepts down further.
- Using more relatable terms and phrases.
- Gathering feedback from participants.
- Looping in their own context and experience.
- Keeping the training grounded in practical community development.

That feedback is important because it points toward a community-led model, not a generic "AI course".

### 5. Identified a broader opportunity through Links

Sam saw potential for this to be useful beyond Seeds of Promise, including with Links partners across Malawi, Uganda, Kenya, and Zambia.

He mentioned the possibility of using the material with African Links partners and asked whether I would record a short introductory session for a partner summit. I did not respond at the time because of work and grief, but the opportunity itself showed that Links may be a useful route to reach other community leaders.

## What Seeds of Promise / Similar Communities Need

The emerging need is not just "AI training". The bigger need appears to be the combination of:

- Reliable connectivity, especially Starlink.
- Better tech infrastructure around existing computer rooms.
- Practical support setting up hardware and systems.
- Basic computer and tech skills for local people.
- AI coaching that is contextual, hands-on, and accessible.
- Local champions who can keep things going after I leave.

Seeds of Promise already has a computer room, but the current setup is not where it needs to be. The strongest immediate infrastructure need appears to be connectivity, particularly Starlink.

The core opportunity is to connect infrastructure and coaching:

> Internet access + usable devices + local training + AI coaching = a practical platform for learning, development, and support.

## Future Plan

### Phase 1: April / May 2026 - One-Week Scoping and Delivery Trip

The first step would be a one-week trip to Malawi, most likely in April or May 2026.

This should be framed as part scoping, part delivery.

The purpose would be to:

- Deliver immediate value to Seeds of Promise.
- Understand the current tech setup properly.
- Assess what Starlink / connectivity would unlock.
- Run practical AI coaching with local leaders and/or learners.
- Identify 1-2 local champions who could keep things moving.
- Work out whether the model is replicable in other communities.
- Gather stories, footage, and evidence that could support fundraising.

This trip should not be framed as "I am coming to solve everything". It should be framed as:

> I want to understand what would actually help, deliver something useful while I am there, and see whether there is a repeatable model worth building.

### Possible Team For The Week

I would like to consider bringing a small group of people who are tech and/or AI experienced.

Potential roles:

- AI coaching / training support.
- Hardware and setup support.
- Connectivity / Starlink setup support.
- Documentation and playbook creation.
- Videography / storytelling for fundraising.

I have a friend who is a videographer who could potentially come and capture the work, if Seeds of Promise and Sam are comfortable with that. The aim would not be to create a glossy charity video for its own sake, but to document the work well enough to help fundraise and explain the opportunity to tech companies and other supporters.

### Phase 2: October 2026 - One-Month Sabbatical Trip

During my sabbatical, likely around October 2026, the next step would be a one-month trip to Malawi.

The purpose would be to take what was learned from Seeds of Promise and test whether it can work in other communities connected to Sam / Links.

Potential goals:

- Work with 2-3 additional communities.
- Support Starlink / connectivity planning or setup where realistic.
- Deliver adapted AI coaching sessions.
- Train local champions in each community.
- Build a repeatable setup and training playbook.
- Understand the real barriers: cost, customs, maintenance, language, training, adoption.
- Decide whether this is a meaningful one-off project, a repeatable programme, or the seed of something larger.

## Practical Challenge: Getting Tech Into Malawi

One of the major practical questions is how to get hardware into Malawi.

Open questions:

- Is it better to source laptops, dongles, routers, and other kit in-country?
- Can Starlinks be brought in directly, or should they be sourced locally / regionally?
- What paperwork is required for donated equipment?
- Are there customs duties or import restrictions that would make bringing kit from the UK difficult?
- Do Links or Seeds of Promise already have experience with this?
- Is there a trusted local person or organisation who should handle procurement?

This needs Sam's advice before any serious equipment plan is made.

## Risks and Things To Be Careful About

- Avoid assuming the answer before listening properly.
- Do not make this a "tech people arrive with answers" project.
- Avoid creating dependency on me personally.
- Make sure local leaders own the model.
- Do not overcomplicate the first trip.
- Be realistic about maintenance, replacement costs, and ongoing internet fees.
- Be thoughtful about filming, consent, and the dignity of the people involved.
- Do not confuse a powerful personal experience with a scalable model until it has been tested.
