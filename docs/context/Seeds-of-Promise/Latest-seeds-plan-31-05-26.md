# Seeds of Promise x AstraJax

**Working title:** Context-aware AI for communities that are ready to move.  
**Status:** Pitch deck source narrative.  
**Date:** 31 May 2026.  
**Owner:** Matthew.

---

## The One-Line Vision

Seeds of Promise x AstraJax is a philanthropic proof point for a bigger belief: when communities have the right foundation, AI can give them access to knowledge, coaching, and specialist support that would otherwise be out of reach.

This is not a generic AI training project. It is a practical model for combining connectivity, local trust, contextual coaching, and AI agents that are shaped around the people using them.

---

## The Pitch

Seeds of Promise already has the most important ingredients: a trusted community, local leadership, a clear appetite for technology, and an existing vision for a computer centre.

What is missing is the foundation layer that turns ambition into access:

- Reliable internet.
- Stable power.
- Working devices.
- Practical training.
- Local champions.
- AI tools that understand the community's language, context, and needs.

AstraJax can help build that missing layer.

The first opportunity is Seeds of Promise in Malawi. The wider opportunity is a repeatable model for community organisations across Links-connected partners and beyond.

---

## The Problem

Underserved communities are not short of ambition, talent, or leadership. They are often short of access.

That access gap shows up in practical ways:

- Children and young people have limited access to digital learning.
- Community leaders rely on word of mouth, long journeys, or scarce expert advice.
- Local projects such as farming, greenhouses, tailoring, education, and fundraising need specialist guidance that is hard to reach.
- Charities and local teams often have strong ideas but limited support turning those ideas into plans, proposals, and fundable stories.
- Generic technology training does not travel well unless it is adapted to the language, culture, and lived reality of the people using it.

AI does not solve this on its own. Dropping a powerful tool into a low-connectivity environment creates more friction, not less.

The opportunity is to build the conditions around the tool, then fit the tool to the community.

## The Answer

Seeds of Promise x AstraJax creates a foundation for practical, context-aware AI use in community settings.

The model has three connected parts.

### 1. The Infrastructure Layer

Help communities get the basics in place:

- Connectivity, likely through Starlink or the most appropriate local option.
- Power resilience, potentially including solar.
- Usable laptops, tablets, routers, and supporting equipment.
- A working computer-room setup that can be maintained locally.
- Clear ownership for maintenance, access, and safeguarding.

This is the part funders can understand quickly: help a specific community get connected and equipped.

### 2. The Context Layer

Once the foundation exists, AstraJax helps turn AI from a general tool into local support.

That means:

- Training local champions.
- Capturing the community's real needs, language, constraints, and goals.
- Building simple AI agents for specific jobs.
- Adapting coaching and guidance into forms that are usable in the local context.
- Keeping humans in control, with AI supporting rather than replacing local judgement.

This is where the AstraJax thesis becomes visible: useful AI depends on context.

### 3. The Agentic Support Layer

The distinctive AstraJax layer is what happens after the room is connected and the local context has been captured.

This is not generic AI training and it is not loose access to a chatbot. It is a small toolkit of narrow, context-aware agents built around specific community jobs:

- A learning support agent for young people.
- A farming and greenhouse guidance agent.
- A fundraising and proposal-writing agent.
- A small-enterprise planning agent.
- A community-leadership planning agent.

Each agent should carry the local context: language, goals, available resources, constraints, cultural references, and the practical realities of Seeds of Promise. The point is not to make local leaders technical. The point is to bring specialist support closer to them, in a form they can actually use.

This is the cleanest crossover with AstraJax:

> AstraJax structures the work. The context layer structures the local reality. Agents use both.

In a commercial team, the foundation is clean workflows, trusted data, clear roles, and human approval.

At Seeds of Promise, the foundation is connectivity, power, devices, local champions, and community context.

The method is the same: build the boring layer first, then give people bounded agents that help with real decisions while humans stay in control.

## What This Could Unlock

The initial use cases are practical and immediate.

### Education

Young people could use AI to support learning, ask questions, practise digital skills, and access explanations adapted to their level and language.

### Farming And Greenhouses

Community leaders could use an agent trained around local farming goals, greenhouse setup, climate conditions, and available resources. Instead of travelling hours to find advice, they could get structured guidance at the point of need.

### Fundraising

Local teams could write their vision in their own words, including in Chichewa, and use an agent to shape it into partner updates, funding proposals, pitch materials, or donor-ready narratives.

### Small Enterprise

Projects such as tailoring businesses or local trading initiatives could get help with simple business planning, pricing, stock thinking, customer messaging, and grant applications.

### Community Leadership

Village elders and local leaders could use AI to structure plans, compare options, prepare meetings, explain ideas, and access guidance that would otherwise depend on external experts.

The principle is simple: bring the specialist closer.

## The Programme Model

The first version should be deliberately focused.

### Phase 1: Seeds of Promise Pilot

Use Seeds of Promise as the flagship pilot site.

Objectives:

- Confirm the connectivity and power requirements.
- Assess the existing computer-room setup.
- Identify the minimum equipment needed.
- Run practical AI and digital-skills sessions.
- Identify local champions.
- Capture real use cases from the community.
- Build a small set of first agents around those use cases.
- Document the playbook.

### Phase 2: Local Agent Toolkit

Create a small set of context-aware agents designed for local needs. This is the agentic layer: narrow helpers that can ask better questions, adapt examples, and turn local knowledge into usable coaching, plans, and materials.

Potential first agents:

- Learning support agent for young people.
- Farming and greenhouse guidance agent.
- Fundraising and proposal-writing agent.
- Small-business planning agent.
- Community-leadership planning agent.

Each agent should be narrow, practical, and grounded in the community's language and reality.

The value is not that the agent knows everything. The value is that it has been deliberately shaped around Seeds of Promise, so it can package outside knowledge into a form that fits the people, the resources, and the decisions in front of them.

### Phase 3: Replication Through Links

If the Seeds of Promise pilot works, test the model with other Links-connected communities.

The aim is not to copy-paste a course. The aim is to copy the method:

1. Build the foundation.
2. Understand the context.
3. Train local champions.
4. Deploy narrow, context-aware agents.
5. Measure what changes.
6. Adapt before scaling.

## The Funding Story

The fundraising proposition is concrete.

This is not "fund AI in Africa", which is too vague.

It is:

> Help connect and equip a specific community in Malawi, capture the local context, then build practical AI agents that support education, farming, fundraising, local enterprise, and community leadership.

In simple terms, funders support three layers:

1. Access - connectivity, power, devices, and a usable computer centre.
2. Context - local champions, community needs, language, constraints, and goals.
3. Agents - narrow AI helpers that turn that context into practical coaching and materials.

Funders could support:

- Starlink or local connectivity.
- Solar or power resilience.
- Laptops, tablets, routers, and supporting equipment.
- Training materials.
- Local champion development.
- Context capture and agent design.
- Travel and delivery costs.
- Documentation, filming, and playbook creation.
- Ongoing support for the first agent toolkit.

The story is strong because it is specific, practical, and already rooted in relationship.

Seeds of Promise is not an abstract beneficiary. It is a known community with an existing vision, existing leadership, and a clear next layer of need.

## What Success Looks Like

Success is not measured by how impressive the technology sounds.

Success means:

- The computer centre becomes meaningfully more useful.
- Local leaders can access guidance without waiting for external experts.
- Young people gain practical confidence with digital tools.

- Local champions can keep the work moving without Matthew becoming the bottleneck.
