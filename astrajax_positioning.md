## 1. Core Positioning

**Stop running high-value work through low-leverage tools.**

AstraJax helps commercial teams turn messy workflows, scattered data, manual admin, and trapped domain knowledge into **AI-ready operating systems** - built with the people who understand the work best.

The belief:

> **Domain experts do not need to become technical. With AI, they can become architects.**

AstraJax is not about installing more AI tools. It is about building the conditions where AI can do useful work: clean data, clear workflows, trusted systems, trained people, bounded agents, and human judgement still in control.

## 2. The Problem

Growing commercial teams often reach the same breaking point:

- important work lives across Sheets, Slack, Gmail, WhatsApp, Notion, and people's heads
- operators spend too much time copying, chasing, reconciling, and reporting
- leaders lack reliable visibility into what is happening and why
- AI experiments are exciting but unreliable because the underlying data and workflows are messy
- the business depends on heroic people instead of repeatable systems

The result is predictable: high-value people spend too much time doing low-leverage work, and the company cannot safely automate because the operating layer is not ready.

AstraJax is built from a live proof point, not a theory.

Matthew Hopkinson is a non-technical commercial leader: ex-professional actor (RADA), then Head of Direct Sales for a ~£8.1m channel. He has **never handwritten a line of code**. Working entirely with AI on top of clean operational data, he built a production operating system that ran a real commercial function at scale.

That matters because the AstraJax promise is not "look how technical we are." It is:

> **A non-technical commercial leader did this with AI, on top of clean data - so your domain experts can too.**

Four advantages make the offer hard to copy:

1. **Operator credibility.** Matthew owned the P&L and team the system served. He understands the commercial, operational, and emotional reality from the inside.
2. **Domain-expert architecture.** The work translates operational judgement into records, fields, statuses, rules, prompts, approvals, and workflows.
3. **Adoption culture.** AstraJax does not assume people will use new systems because they are "better." Adoption is engineered through trust, training, visible value, safety, play, and role-specific workflows.
4. **Maintainability.** The work includes handover, feedback loops, context governance, and human-gated change flows so the system does not rot after launch.

Underlying all four: **the hard part of AI is rarely just the technology. It is the data, the workflows, and the humans.**

## 6. Method

**1. Diagnose the mess.** Identify where high-value work is trapped in low-leverage tools.

**2. Build the boring layer.** Clean data, clear workflows, trusted tools, and role-specific interfaces. This is the unglamorous foundation that makes AI useful.

**3. Turn domain experts into architects.** Translate their judgement into structured systems: records, fields, statuses, rules, prompts, approvals, and workflows.

**4. Add bounded agents.** Narrow jobs, clear handoffs, clean context, human approval, and audit trails. Agents on messy data become confident chaos machines.

**5. Engineer adoption.** Trust, training, value, and safety are designed into delivery. The system should feel useful, understandable, and safe enough that people actually use it.

**6. Hand over and maintain.** Create ownership rituals, feedback loops, and context governance so the team can keep improving the system without AstraJax becoming the permanent interface.

## 8. Flagship Proof Point - Butternut Box

> **From Gmail, WhatsApp, Notion, and Google Sheets to an operating system.**

The Butternut Box Direct Sales story proves the thesis: a non-technical commercial leader, working entirely with AI on top of clean data, shipped a production operating layer that gave a real team real leverage.

The build:

- an operating system for a **~£8.1m** Direct Sales channel
- 3 P&Ls, ~15 office FTE, ~90 field salespeople
- ~64k annual acquisitions target

The sequencing matters:

- **~12 months on the boring layer first:** data cleaning, workflow design, system architecture, and operational discipline
- **Then the build moved fast:** ~9 production interface extensions and ~556 TypeScript/React files in roughly a month
- **First agent fleet in two weeks:** only possible because the foundation already existed
- **None of it hand-coded by Matthew**

Measured outcomes and proof:

- **~£180k/year** saved through better travel planning
- **~3,000 hours/year** operational capacity absorbed at scale
- labour-model transformation driven by categorised performance data, not gut feel
- role-specific interfaces for different personas
- agent fleet with bounded jobs and human approval
- governed bug, fix, ship, reporter sign-off, and weekly-summary loop

Patterns that made it work:

- **Bounded agents:** link -> propose -> human approves -> execute
- **Human judgement preserved:** agents take the sludge; humans keep the meaning
- **Adoption by design:** training hub, sandbox, engagement leaderboards, XP, and characterful agents people want to use
- **Personality as adoption infrastructure:** a commercially serious system with a tiny sitcom living inside it
- **Maintenance built in:** feedback loops, human gates, audit trails, and context hygiene
