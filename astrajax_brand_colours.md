# AstraJax - Brand Colours

**Status:** Working colour direction for AstraJax visual identity.
**Selected route:** Nocturne Orchard — with **Pale Cream** as the primary public surface (Tara Lee / website).
**Audience:** Matthew, designers, AI assistants, and anyone creating AstraJax visual assets.
**Companion docs:** `astrajax_positioning.md`, `astrajax_ops_brief.md`, and `AGENTS.md`.
**Last updated:** 30 May 2026

## 1. Direction

**Nocturne Orchard** is the overall colour territory for AstraJax — warm, mossy, human, operational.

Within that territory, **Tara Lee's preferred primary** is the **pale cream** editorial surface from the website architecture mockups:

> [AstraJax — Website Architecture Mockups](https://hyperagent.com/s/wRr01C_HoXwlPFnlhgFYCA?v=2)

Use that cream route for:

- the public website and marketing
- client-facing one-pagers and decks
- the **surface** — overview, first impression, editorial calm

Use **night mode** (dark moss / Nocturne Orchard) for:

- **deep dives** — drawing people below the surface into detail
- review queues, audit trails, change logs, agent ops, context libraries
- anywhere the job is "go deeper" rather than "stay at overview"

Night mode is not "the scary internal tool look." It is the visual cue that you have left the brochure and entered the operating layer — focused, immersive, detail-rich.

The feel should be:

- warm and premium without becoming corporate-cold
- human and characterful without becoming playful or childish
- inspired by Butternut Box warmth, but more editorial and operational
- cyberpunk-adjacent only in the restraint, not in neon colour

The core idea:

> **Surface:** daylight on cream paper — trusted, legible, founder-led. **Below the surface:** night mode — moss and parchment, drawing people into detail. Same brand, two depths. Cream invites; dark immerses.

## 3. Colour Roles

### Pale Cream (primary — Tara Lee / website)

Use as the **default page background** for the website, marketing, and cream-themed interfaces.

This is the editorial "paper" surface — not yellow Buttermilk. It should feel like quality stationery: calm, readable, founder-led.

Pair with **Ink** text and **Terracotta** for primary actions. Do not put Deep Moss behind large public page areas unless the section is deliberately atmospheric.

### Cream Paper

Use for cards, mockup frames, and raised panels sitting on Pale Cream.

Slightly lighter than the page — almost white-warm, not pure white.

### Cream Inset

Use for form fields, code wells, and detail panels that need a touch more depth than Cream Paper.

### Ink

Use as the default text colour on cream surfaces.

Warmer and softer than pure black. Headlines and body both sit in Ink or a muted Ink derivative — not Parchment Dim (that token is for dark backgrounds).

### Terracotta

Use for primary CTAs on cream: "Book an Audit", main action buttons, Clive `//` accents.

This is deeper and more editorial than Burnt Apricot. On cream, Terracotta is allowed as a solid button fill — unlike Burnt Apricot on dark, where it should stay smaller.

### Sage Signal (on cream)

Use `#6E7B52` on cream surfaces for live / approved / active states.

The dark-surface Sage (`#9AA77A`) is the same family — slightly lighter for contrast on moss backgrounds.

### Deep Moss

Use as the primary background for **night mode** — deep-dive interfaces where the user is going below the surface into detail.

Examples: context review, intake queues, change logs, agent fleet, audit trails, hash chains, anything that says "you are inside the system now."

Also use for atmospheric hero moments and presentation title slides when you want drama without asking the user to read dense detail.

Deep Moss carries focus and immersion. It tells the user they have moved from overview to operating depth.

### Graphite Ink

Use as the structural dark: nav bars, card frames, modals, side panels, dividers, and product UI chrome on the dark route.

Graphite Ink stops Deep Moss from feeling too botanical. It makes the palette feel more operational and less outdoorsy.

### Blueberry Black

Use sparingly for maximum depth: footer blocks, dark rails, code-like areas, or secondary surfaces that need to sit behind the main moss layer.

Avoid using Blueberry Black everywhere or the identity may become generic dark SaaS.

### Parchment Dim

Use as the default text colour on **dark** surfaces only.

This is warmer than white, which keeps the brand from feeling sterile.

### Buttermilk

Use for warm emphasis on dark surfaces: small badges, important numbers, key labels, selected states, or diagram highlights.

On cream surfaces, prefer Cream Paper or a light Sage wash instead — Buttermilk reads too yellow on Pale Cream.

### Burnt Apricot

Use for human warmth on **dark** surfaces: Clive notes, founder-led moments, small editorial marks, quote details, or occasional CTA support.

On cream, use **Terracotta** for primary CTAs instead.

Do not use Burnt Apricot as the main status colour. It works when it feels human, not when it feels like an alert.

### Sage Signal (on dark)

Use for system state on dark backgrounds: active agent, live process, approved, synced, connected, or ready.

Sage Signal should stay quiet. It is a signal colour, not a hero colour.

## 5. Application Rules

### Use This

**On cream (surface):**

- Pale Cream as the main page background.
- Cream Paper for cards and raised panels.
- Ink for readable text.
- Terracotta for primary CTAs and Clive accents.
- Sage Signal (`#6E7B52`) for live / approved states.

**On night mode (deep dive):**

- Deep Moss as the main background — signals "below the surface."
- Graphite Ink to frame panels, cards, nav, and modals.
- Parchment Dim for readable text.
- Buttermilk for warm emphasis and small branded moments.
- Burnt Apricot for human notes, character, and warmth.
- Sage Signal (`#9AA77A`) for live/active/approved system states.

### Avoid This

- Plasma cyan, teal, or electric mint.
- Bright amber or infrared as a system signal.
- Brown-heavy surfaces next to moss.
- Pure black and pure white unless forced by a specific medium.
- Neon gradients, chrome effects, or generic cyberpunk purple.
- Using Burnt Apricot as a large block colour on dark.
- Using Buttermilk as a page background — too yellow; use **Pale Cream** instead.
- Defaulting every interface to Deep Moss when the brief is public-facing or website-aligned.
- Using night mode for marketing pages or first-touch overview — save it for when people are ready to go deeper.
- Using cream for dense audit/review surfaces where immersion and detail are the job — that is night mode territory.

## 7. Voice Of The Palette

This colour system should support the AstraJax positioning:

> Domain expertise turned into AI-ready operating systems.

The colours should make AstraJax feel:

- serious enough for commercial leaders
- warm enough to be founder-led
- distinctive enough not to look like another AI SaaS tool
- technical enough to imply systems, agents, and operating layers
- restrained enough to feel trusted

**On cream (surface):** the test is whether it feels like editorial quality paper — calm, legible, human — not a generic SaaS dashboard or a yellow-beige template.

**On night mode (deep dive):** the test is whether it draws you into detail without feeling like a gaming UI or a bank vault. Immersive, warm-dark, operational. If it starts looking like a gardening brand, the moss is too dominant or not framed by enough Graphite Ink.
