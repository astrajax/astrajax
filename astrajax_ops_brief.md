## 3. Claim-Control Rules

These rules keep the positioning credible.

### Lead with

- Matthew has never handwritten a line of code.
- The platform was built with AI, on top of clean data and a year of system-architecture work.
- The rare thing is who did it, how it was sequenced, and that it runs in production.
- The hard part of useful AI is data, workflows, adoption, and maintenance.

### Always pair

- "Never written a line of code" with **"with AI, on top of clean data."**
- "Built fast" with **"after approximately 12 months of boring-layer work."**
- "AI agents" with **"bounded scope, human approval, and audit trail."**
- "Adoption culture" with concrete proof: training hub, sandbox, engagement leaderboard, XP, characterful agents.

### Do not over-claim

- Do not present Matthew as an engineer.
- Do not imply the tech stack is novel computer science.
- Do not imply every team can skip technical expertise forever.
- Do not claim broad enterprise change-management or executive-coaching expertise unless developed later.
- Do not turn AstraJax into a generic Airtable build shop, AI consultancy, CRM implementer, or chatbot installer.

### Canonical proof numbers

Use these unless Matthew updates them:

- Channel spend: **~£8.1m**
- Scope: 3 P&Ls, ~15 office FTE, ~90 field salespeople
- 2026 acquisition target: **~64k**
- Interface proof: ~9 production interface extensions, ~556 TypeScript/React files
- Sequence: ~12 months boring layer -> roughly 1 month interface build -> first agent fleet in 2 weeks
- Outcomes: **~£180k/year** travel saving and **~3,000 hours/year** operational capacity at scale

Talk-track figures such as 1,500 employees, 120 sellers, and 7 markets are useful narrative context for Butternut company scale, but the numbers above are canonical for Direct Sales proof.

## 4. Launch Asset Rules

### Website

Use `astrajax_positioning.md` for source language, but make the public site sharper and less caveated. The site should not read like an internal strategy doc.

Website must communicate:

- high-value work is trapped in low-leverage tools
- AstraJax builds the operating layer that makes AI useful
- domain experts can become architects
- Matthew's zero-code-to-production story is proof
- adoption and maintenance are built into the approach

### Butternut Box case study

The case study should carry the narrative weight that the website cannot:

- before state: Sheets / WhatsApp / Gmail / Notion, fragmented data, manual coordination
- boring layer: clean data, workflows, role-specific interfaces
- acceleration: 556 files in roughly a month, agent fleet in two weeks
- outcomes: cost, time, labour-model insight, adoption
- lesson: the foundation made the speed possible

### Commercial OS Audit one-pager

The audit should feel practical, not theoretical:

- diagnose workflow drag
- map data/tool fragmentation
- identify AI-readiness gaps
- prioritise first operating-layer sprint
- show the path to bounded AI and context governance

## 6. Guidance for AI Assistants

Use `astrajax_positioning.md` as the source of truth for what AstraJax is. Use `AGENTS.md` for founder background and proof detail. Use this file for execution and guardrails.

### Default framing

> **AstraJax helps commercial teams turn domain expertise into AI-ready operating systems. Clive maintains the context environment those systems and agents rely on.**

### When drafting

- Address Matthew as **Matthew**, not Matt.
- Keep language grounded, specific, and commercially useful.
- Preserve the founder story, but avoid making the whole business sound like autobiography.
- Use proof points only when they strengthen the buyer's confidence.
- Translate internal humour into polished warmth; do not flatten the creativity out of the brand.
- Be clear that adoption and maintenance are part of the system design.

### Do not drift into

- pet businesses
- generic lead generation
- broad AI consulting
- "Matthew builds Airtable bases"
- pure no-code implementation
- chatbot installation
- vague future-of-work thought leadership

### Sensitivity guardrail

Keep personal finances, medical specifics, bereavement detail, and private HR/team detail out of shareable docs. Use behavioural takeaways only when they help the work.
